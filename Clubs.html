Kaffeinated Kodemonkey offers two (2) coding clubs for kids

Kaffeinated Kodemonkey believe that an understanding of programming languages is increasingly important in the modern world. We believe that all kids should have the opportunity at an early age to learn these skills and that nobody should be denied to do so!

Both Clubs are held at CNCC Rangely Campus at 6:30 pm - 7:30 pm Monday's and Wednesday.

Anyone can attenc either club - tipical age range is 7-17. There is no food served so please eat ahead of time

Don't Miss our monthly Kodemonkey-Jam Session: These sessions will be held on last Wed of the month in place of our club meetings. Your sprouting kodemonkey will demonstrate what they are learning in the club either through a topic discussion or show-n-tell. This is a great time for anyone interested to come see what we do best.

Kodemokey-Dojo - Free

This club follow the CoderDojer movement out of Ireland. Our goal is to help build a global network of free, volunteer-led, community-based programming clubs for young people. You will learn skills such as:

  • Gaming & Animation - Through Scratch
  • Website Development - Learn HTML, CSS, JavaScript, & PHP
  • High-level programming - Python & Ruby
  • Electronics with Programming - Raspberry PI & Arduino
  • Application Development - For mobile aps

You will explore technology in an informal, creative, and social environment.This club is self paced you can work in groups or on your own.

Kodemonkey-Google - $50 mon / per students

Google CS First is a step up form the KodemonkeyDojo club above. This is a curriculum based club that focuses on the computer science of programming. The goal behind Google CS First is to allow students to progress on a path that can expand their mind and offer opportunities beyond the classroom. These students can crossover to the KodemonyDojo club as leaders on a project or a mentor, or advance on to building a profile and helping with freelance work at Kaffeinated Kodemonkey if there is a desire to learn more and gain real world experience.

You will learn skills in the following areas:

  • Story Telling - In Storytelling, students use computer science to tell fun and interactive stories.
  • Friends - Friends emphasizes teamwork allowing club members to tell a story of how met & imagine a company together.
  • Fashion & Design - Students learn how computer science & technology are used in the fashion industry.
  • Art - Students create animations, interactive artwork, photograph filters, and other exciting, artistic projects.
  • Social Media - Students create fun social media style applications and games while learning about the computer science concepts.
  • Sports - Students use computer science to simulate sports, make their own commercial, & create commentary for a sporting event.
  • Music & Sound - Students use the computer to play music, create a video, & build an interactive music display.
  • Game Design -Students learn basic video game coding concepts by making different types of games, including racing, platform etc..
  • Animation - Students create fun & complex animated projects. This is an advanced curriculum, which means it teaches new concepts that are recommended for students who have already participated in at least two other CS First themes.

Join us Pick a Club and have fun!
